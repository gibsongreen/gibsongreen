Under Dr. Buzasi, I research using the program gPhoton. gPhoton is a Python program written to sift through GALEXs (Galaxy Evolution Explorer) time and location stamped photon data that was previously unviewable. Using the program, I go through star sources provided by Dr. Buzasi and develop light curves for the targets. For the Stellar Research team, I have also begun redesigning the current fgcstellar.org website.
From my high school transcripts and test scores, I was offered a seat in FGCU's Honors College immediadetly upon acceptance into the University. In my freshman year: I lived in the Honors Housing Community and developed relationships with my peers in the Honors College. I took Honors Sequence Courses in: Research Symposium & Planet Hunters. I applied to be an Honors College Mentor in the Spring of 2018 and received the position. In the position, I represented the school and the Honors College in helping develop and framiliarize incoming freshman Honors College Students through mentorship. I held this position throughout my sophmore year, holding the highest leadership position in the Honors College.
FGCU Honors course titled Planet Hunters was taught by Dr. Derek Buzasi. The first semester developed the class knowledge of general Astronomical and Astrophysical topics. The second semester followed with a research aspect where teams analyzed 200 light curves from Keplers Campaigns 15 & 16 data using Dr. Buzasis AstroDev tool. In doing so, my teammate and I discovered one candidate exoplanet, one rotating star, and two binary stars. To conclude the course, I represented my class, as Dr. Buzasi and I travel to Denver for the 232nd American Astronomical Society Conference. We presented our classes discoveries
